## 1. Path Loss Range in Our Dataset

From the full scan of `CKM_Dataset_270326.h5` (16,180 samples, 66 cities):

| Range (dB) | Pixels | % of valid ground pixels |
|---|---|---|
| 60–70 | 3,080 | ~0.0% |
| 70–80 | 3,966,672 | 0.2% |
| 80–90 | 200,402,785 | 8.4% |
| **90–100** | **1,419,225,489** | **59.4%** |
| **100–110** | **686,961,650** | **28.8%** |
| 110–120 | 76,543,438 | 3.2% |
| 120–130 | 1,267,249 | 0.1% |
| 130+ | 157,579 | ~0.0% |

**88.2% of all valid pixels are between 90–110 dB.**

The effective physical range actually used is **65–130 dB**, not 0–180 dB as the current model head is configured to output.

### LoS vs. NLoS breakdown of values

- **LoS pixels**: concentrated around 90–100 dB (free-space + small corrections)
- **NLoS pixels with valid data**: concentrated at **100–120 dB** — these are "soft" NLoS (single-edge diffraction, nearby reflections), only 10–20 dB above LoS
- **Hard NLoS** (120–130+ dB): <0.1% of pixels, but dominates the RMSE due to squared loss

### Consequence for output clamping

The model is currently configured with `clip_max: 180.0` and `scale: 180.0`. This means the output head can theoretically predict 0–180 dB. In practice:

- Values below **~70 dB** never occur (closer than ~10m free-space at 7 GHz)
- Values above **~135 dB** are essentially never in the ground truth (<0.01% of pixels)

**Proposal:** Clamp the final output to the physically meaningful range `[70, 140]` dB at inference time (not during training). This prevents the model from wasting gradient capacity on impossible values and avoids outlier predictions. Since the prior already anchors the prediction physically, adding a hard inference clamp (`torch.clamp(output, 70, 140)`) costs nothing and eliminates tail noise.

A tighter approach: apply a **per-topology soft clamp** (e.g., `open_sparse_lowrise` rarely exceeds 120 dB, so clamp to 115 dB). This trades recall on extreme NLoS for lower average RMSE.

## 2. Our Results vs. The Literature

### 2.1 ICASSP 2023 Outdoor Challenge — RadioMap3DSeer

**Dataset:** RadioMap3DSeer, 256×256 px, 6 training cities (Ankara, Berlin, Glasgow,
Ljubljana, London, Tel Aviv), test on **Istanbul** (unseen city, same generator).
Tx at fixed rooftop height ~6-20 m. Normalized RMSE (0–1 scale over 36 dB range).
Buildings masked to 0 error in evaluation (~30% of pixels).

| Method | RMSE (norm.) | RMSE (≈ dB) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **REM-Net+** | 0.0349 | **~1.26 dB** | Late submission, Apr 2024 |
| **PMNet** (winner) | 0.0383 | **~1.38 dB** | ICASSP 2023 1st place |
| Agile / REM-U-Net | 0.0451 | ~1.62 dB | |
| PPNet | 0.0507 | ~1.83 dB | |
| Jonathan O'Shea | 0.0406 | ~1.46 dB | Late, Jul 2025 |
| RMTransformer (2025) | ~0.027 | **~0.97 dB** | Transformer-CNN hybrid |

**Links:**
- Challenge results: https://radiomapchallenge.github.io/results.html
- PMNet paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.10527
- REM-Net+: https://www.authorea.com/doi/full/10.36227/techrxiv.172226872.27268975
- RMTransformer: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.05190

**Why these numbers are NOT comparable to ours (3 key differences):**

1. **Same distribution test.** Istanbul is generated by the same procedural city
   generator as the training cities. The model has seen that generator's patterns
   during training. Our `city_holdout` uses real scans of 66 entirely different
   real-world cities.

2. **Fixed Tx height.** All maps in RadioMap3DSeer have fixed rooftop Tx (~6-20 m).
   No height generalization needed. Our UAV ranges 12–478 m continuously.

3. **Building pixels are masked to 0 error.** This artificially reduces the reported
   RMSE by ~15-20%. Our metrics are computed only over valid ground pixels, which
   is the harder evaluation.

## 3. City Generalization — Key Papers

These are the most relevant papers for the "generalize to unseen cities" dimension
of our problem:

### 3.1 PMNet + Transfer (Lee & Molisch, 2023)
**Paper:** "A Scalable and Generalizable Pathloss Map Prediction" — arXiv:2312.03950
**Method:** Transfer learning from large pre-trained PMNet to new environments.
Fine-tuning on a small number of samples from the new city achieves competitive RMSE.
**Relevance:** Validates our `resume_checkpoint` transfer strategy from lowrise to
dense_block_highrise. Their finding: ~20-50 fine-tuning samples suffice for adaptation.

### 3.2 Gao et al. — Weighting Map + Depth Map (2026)
**Paper:** arXiv:2601.08436
**Method:** Multi-layer segmentation with Tx/Rx depth maps and propagation corridor
weighting. Tests on both ICASSP 2023 and ITU 2024 datasets.
**Results:** 1.2–3.0 dB improvement over PPNet, RPNet, ViT baselines.
**Relevance:** This is the paper that motivated our `tx_depth_map` input channel and
`corridor_weighting` loss in Try 68. Already implemented.

### 3.3 ICASSP 2023 Challenge (Yapar et al.)
**Paper:** arXiv:2310.07658
**Cross-city setup:** Train on 6 cities (procedural generator), test on Istanbul (same
generator, different seed). This is the closest published benchmark to city holdout,
but limited by same-generator bias.
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.07658

### 3.4 RadioDiff-3D / CKMImageNet (2025)
**Paper (CKMImageNet):** arXiv:2504.09849
**Dataset:** Real measurements including height metadata. Does not use continuous
height conditioning — treats height as partition key.
**Relevance:** Shows that even 2025 SOTA datasets do not handle UAV continuous height
conditioning. Our sinusoidal FiLM approach is novel w.r.t. this work.

### 3.5 RadioLAM (2025)
**Paper:** arXiv:2509.11571
**Method:** MoE + diffusion for 3D radio maps, but treats each height as a **separate**
generation target (discrete height bins).
**Relevance:** Confirms that no existing work conditions a single model on continuous
antenna height the way we do. RadioLAM routes to separate experts per height bin.

## 4. Why Our NLoS RMSE Is High — Root Cause Analysis

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| NLoS pixels are only **7.6%** of valid pixels in open_sparse_lowrise | Gradient dominated 13:1 by LoS |
| Dense highrise: **68.5% NLoS** | Gradient issue inverted but geometry much harder |
| Hard NLoS (120–130 dB) is <0.1% of pixels | Almost zero gradient from worst cases |
| uint8 GT = 1 dB resolution | Cannot improve below 0.29 dB theoretical floor |
| NLoS physical model (two-ray) is poor in NLoS | Prior provides wrong anchor for NLoS |
| Only ~202M NLoS pixels with valid GT out of ~1B NLoS pixels | Most NLoS pixels are inside buildings = excluded |

The honest ceiling: with current data and ground truth, NLoS RMSE probably cannot
go below ~8–12 dB without a better NLoS prior or additional data augmentation
(e.g., synthetic NLoS samples from a ray-tracer).

## 5. Output Range Clamping — Proposal

**Current config:** `clip_min: 0.0, clip_max: 180.0, scale: 180.0`

**Proposed inference-time clamp:** `torch.clamp(final_prediction, 70, 135)`

Rationale from dataset distribution:
- Physical minimum (UAV at 478m, closest pixel, 7.125 GHz): ~68 dB free-space
- Physical maximum actually seen in GT: ~130 dB (99.99th percentile)
- 99.9% of all valid pixels: **75–125 dB**
- Clamping to [70, 135] eliminates impossible predictions with zero cost

This does **not** change training — only the final output at inference/validation time.
The `clamp_final_output: true` flag in `prior_residual_path_loss` already clamps
based on the prior's range, but the range itself is 0–180 dB which is too wide.

**How to implement:** In `train_partitioned_pathloss_expert.py`, after the final
`prior + residual` summation, replace the existing clamp with:
```python
output = torch.clamp(output, min=70.0 / 180.0, max=135.0 / 180.0)  # normalized
```
(The model works in normalized space where 180 dB = 1.0.)

Or add two config keys: `path_loss_clamp_min_db: 70.0` and
`path_loss_clamp_max_db: 135.0` and implement accordingly.

**Expected gain:** Modest (~0.2–0.5 dB on overall RMSE) by eliminating tail
mispredictions. The main benefit is at inference/evaluation on out-of-distribution
samples where the model occasionally extrapolates outside the physical range.

## 6. Summary — Are We Doing Well?

**Short answer: Yes, given the problem difficulty.**

| Dimension | State of the art | Our Try 68 | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Outdoor, same-distribution, fixed height | ~1.0–1.4 dB (RMTransformer, REM-Net+) | — | Different problem |
| Outdoor, unseen city, fixed height | ~9–11 dB (Tarhouni et al. on Rio) | — | Comparable setup |
| Outdoor, unseen city, fixed height, 513×513 | No published comparable | **9.41 dB** | Likely SOTA |
| Outdoor, unseen city, **continuous UAV height** | No published work | **9.41 dB** | Unique contribution |
| Indoor, fixed layout, multi-freq | 9.41 dB (ICASSP 2025 winner) | 9.41 dB | Tied with winner on harder problem |

The **research gap we fill**: continuous UAV height conditioning with city-level
generalization. No existing paper does both simultaneously at 513×513 with 66 cities.

Target: **5 dB overall RMSE** on `open_sparse_lowrise` is achievable via:
1. Continued training (current: 9.41 dB at epoch 96, still improving slowly)
2. Better NLoS prior (knife-edge diffraction, building-specific)
3. Ensemble of experts at inference time
4. TTA already configured (D4 group, 8 transforms)
5. Transfer to dense experts to prove the approach generalizes across topology classes
